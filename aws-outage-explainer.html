What is a Race Condition?

A race condition occurs when two or more processes access shared data concurrently, and the final outcome depends on the timing of execution — not the logic. If Process A and Process B both read and write to the same resource without synchronization, the “winner” of the race determines the system’s state — often incorrectly.

The Cascade

Why It Couldn’t Self-Recover: Metastable Failure

Even after DNS was restored in ~2.5 hours, the region didn’t recover. The system entered a metastable failure state — technically running, but stuck in a broken loop. Retry storms from millions of clients overwhelmed recovering services, preventing them from catching up. Engineers had to manually intervene to break the cycle. Total recovery took 15 hours.

Design Takeaways

Validate at Point of Action

Never trust a stale check. Re-validate shared state right before writing — not minutes earlier.

Guard Automated Cleanup

Deletion scripts are loaded guns. Add confirmation checks, soft-deletes, and cooldown windows.

Isolate Failure Domains

If one service dying kills everything downstream, you have a monolith disguised as microservices.

Design for Metastability

Plan for retry storms. Use backoff, circuit breakers, and load shedding so recovery is possible.
